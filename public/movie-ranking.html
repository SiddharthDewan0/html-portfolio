BEST ANIME MOVIES ACCORDING TO SIDDHARTH DEWAN

My top 3 Anime Movies

1.Your Name(Kimi No Na Wa)

Your Name (Kimi No Na Wa), directed by Makoto Shinkai, is a Japanese animated film that follows the story of Mitsuha and Taki, two teenagers who mysteriously start switching bodies and lives.Mitsuha lives in a rural village and dreams of experiencing life in Tokyo,while Taki is a city-dweller with his own aspirations. As they navigate this extraordinaryconnection, they begin leaving messages for each other to cope with the challenges of their switched lives. However, as they grow closer, their experiences take a deeper and more fantastical turn, involving time, destiny,and a connection that spans across space and time. The film beautifully blends themes of love, fate, and identity, along with stunning visuals and an emotionally resonant storyline.

2.Spirited Away

Spirited Away, directed by Hayao Miyazaki, is a Japanese animated fantasy film. The story follows a young girl named Chihiro who becomes trapped in a mysterious and magical world after her parents are transformed into pigs. She must navigate this bizarre and otherworldly realm, which is populated by a variety of spirits, monsters, and supernatural beings. Chihiro works at a bathhouse for spirits to find a way to rescue her parents and return to the human world. As she encounters numerous challenges and makes unexpected friendships, she discovers her own inner strength and resilience. The film explores themes of growing up, self-discovery, and the balance between the human and spirit worlds. With its imaginative visuals and intricate storytelling, "Spirited Away" has become a beloved classic in the world of animation.

3.A Silent Voice(Koe No Katachi)

A Silent Voice, directed by Naoko Yamada, is a Japanese animated film that addresses themes of bullying, redemption, and personal growth. The story centers around Shoya Ishida, a former elementary school bully who targeted a deaf girl named Shoko Nishimiya. Years later, burdened by guilt and remorse, Shoya seeks to make amends for his past actions. He sets out to reconnect with Shoko, who has since transferred schools due to the mistreatment she endured.As Shoya tries to bridge the gap he created between himself and Shoko, he faces his own social isolation and strives for self-forgiveness. The film touches on the complexities of human relationships, communication barriers, and the power of empathy. Through its sensitive portrayal of characters' emotional struggles and personal growth, "A Silent Voice" delves into the impact of one's actions and the potential for redemption and reconciliation.
